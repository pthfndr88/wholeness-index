ARCHETYPE 01 — THE SEEKER
Pattern: High identity, lower character and competence
Tagline: Knowledge without strength.
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING RECOGNITION LINE
(displayed large, italic, serif — first thing they read)

You know yourself. The gap is doing something with that knowing.

─── THE READING ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What this pattern means.

Body:
Your identity score is your strongest dimension.
You have thought carefully about who you are, what you want,
and why it matters. That clarity is real and it is rare.

But clarity about who you are is not the same as being that
person consistently under pressure. The Seeker pattern
emerges when identity runs ahead of the character
and competence needed to act on it.

You can articulate your purpose. What you are still building
is the daily discipline, the courage under pressure, and the
execution habits that translate that purpose into work
the world can feel.

The knowing is not the problem.
The gap is between knowing and being.

─── WHAT THIS COSTS YOU ────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: How this shows up.

Body:
You may find yourself rereading your own notes, revisiting
your purpose statement, refining your direction —
without the forward movement that should follow.

You understand what needs to happen. You are not
consistently doing it.

Others may experience you as thoughtful but inconsistent,
or promising but not yet reliable. That gap between
your internal clarity and your external track record
is the Seeker's signature tension.

─── YOUR FIRST MOVES ───────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: Where to start.

Move 1:
Choose one character discipline — not a value, but a practice.
Courage, for example. Or follow-through. Or early rising.
Practice it daily for thirty days without announcing it.
The point is to build evidence in your own record,
not to perform it for others.

Move 2:
Identify the commitment you have been circling without making.
Make it. Specifically, out loud, to someone who will notice
if you don't follow through. External accountability 
accelerates internal formation.

Move 3:
Stop adding to your vision. Start executing the smallest
possible version of it. The Seeker's growth edge is not
more clarity — it is the tolerance of imperfect action.

─── UPGRADE BRIDGE ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What the full assessment reveals.

Body:
The rapid classifier identifies your pattern.
The full assessment shows you exactly which of the five 
character sub-domains — consistency, ethics, discipline, 
courage, or integrity — is the specific constraint.

For Seekers, that precision matters.
Knowing you need to develop character is not enough.
Knowing that it is specifically discipline, or specifically
courage, gives you somewhere precise to direct the work.

Your Formation Score will also show how the gap between
Identity and the other three domains is affecting your
overall development trajectory — and what closing it
is worth in concrete terms.

ARCHETYPE 02 — THE POWERHOUSE
Pattern: High character and competence, lower identity
Tagline: Strength without direction.
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING RECOGNITION LINE

You are impressively capable. The question is
whether you're pointed at the right things.

─── THE READING ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What this pattern means.

Body:
Your character and competence scores are your strongest
dimensions. You show up consistently, execute reliably,
and deliver at a level that most people around you notice.

The Powerhouse pattern emerges when capability precedes
clarity — when someone has developed real strength before
they have developed a settled sense of what that strength
is for.

Without a clear identity foundation, strong people tend
to optimise for the wrong outcomes. They work hard on
things they were asked to do rather than things they
are called to do. They excel in rooms they should
have left two years ago.

You have the engine. What is less clear is the destination.

─── WHAT THIS COSTS YOU ────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: How this shows up.

Body:
You may feel productive but not purposeful.
Effective but not energised.

You have a strong track record — and a quiet suspicion
that the record doesn't quite reflect what you're
actually capable of when pointed at something
that genuinely matters.

Powerhouses often describe a sense of performing
their potential rather than living it.

─── YOUR FIRST MOVES ───────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: Where to start.

Move 1:
Review your calendar for the last four weeks. Mark in green
the activities that energised you. Mark in red the ones
that drained you. Look at the ratio. The pattern will
tell you something your job description won't.

Move 2:
Ask yourself: if I were two years into the work that most
matters to me, what would I be doing on a Tuesday afternoon?
That question is not hypothetical. It is directional.
Write the answer down before you qualify it into abstraction.

Move 3:
Identify one thing you are doing excellently that
you do not actually care about. Begin the process
of exiting it — not immediately, but deliberately.
Powerhouses grow by subtraction as much as addition.

─── UPGRADE BRIDGE ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What the full assessment reveals.

Body:
For Powerhouses, the full assessment is most useful
in the Identity domain — specifically the sub-domains
of purpose, values clarity, and narrative.

Understanding which of these is most underdeveloped 
gives you a specific place to do the inner work, rather 
than approaching identity as a general project.

Your Alignment Index will also be revealing.
Powerhouses typically show high competence scores
with significant misalignment between identity and impact —
a structural gap the classifier can identify but not fully
measure without the complete instrument.

ARCHETYPE 03 — THE HIDDEN GEM
Pattern: High identity, character, and competence — lower impact
Tagline: Potential without visibility.
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING RECOGNITION LINE

Your work is good. The problem is that
the right people don't know it yet.

─── THE READING ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What this pattern means.

Body:
Your identity, character, and competence scores show
strong development across the first three dimensions.
You are self-aware, principled, and genuinely skilled.

The Hidden Gem pattern emerges when the internal work
is done — but the external translation hasn't followed.
Your contribution isn't reaching the people and contexts
it should reach.

This is not a skills problem. It is not a character problem.
It is a visibility and positioning problem — and those
are behavioural, not developmental. They require 
different action than the work you've already done.

The barrier is not who you are.
It is how consistently you let that be seen.

─── WHAT THIS COSTS YOU ────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: How this shows up.

Body:
You may feel overlooked in rooms you are qualified to lead.
You may watch opportunities go to people you know are
less capable — and struggle to understand why.

You may have a belief, somewhere close to the surface,
that good work should announce itself.
That self-promotion is somehow beneath the quality
of what you do.

That belief is costing you the impact you are capable of.

─── YOUR FIRST MOVES ───────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: Where to start.

Move 1:
This week, do one thing to make your work visible.
Share an insight with someone who matters.
Speak up in a meeting where you would normally let
it pass. Write the thing you've been drafting in your head.
One visible action. Not a campaign — a habit.

Move 2:
Identify three people who are in rooms you should be in.
Not to network — to offer something specific and useful.
Hidden Gems unlock doors by demonstrating value,
not by announcing ambition.

Move 3:
Notice where you are editing yourself before you speak.
The thought that says "they already know this" or
"someone else will say it." That thought is the constraint.
Practice saying the thing anyway, once per week,
in a context that matters.

─── UPGRADE BRIDGE ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What the full assessment reveals.

Body:
The Hidden Gem's full assessment is most diagnostic
in the Impact domain — specifically the sub-domains
of recognition, opportunity creation, and influence.

These sub-domains distinguish between the person who
is invisible because they haven't been seen yet,
and the person who has a structural behaviour pattern
that actively prevents visibility.

The distinction matters enormously for what you do next.
Your Wholeness Score will also reveal whether this is
a formation issue or a strategy issue — and that
determines whether the work is internal or external.

ARCHETYPE 04 — THE FRAGMENTED
Pattern: Inconsistent scores across all four dimensions
Tagline: Scattered energy.
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING RECOGNITION LINE

You have more capability than this result shows.
You're in transition — and that is both the challenge
and the opportunity.

─── THE READING ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What this pattern means.

Body:
The Fragmented pattern does not mean underdeveloped.
It means that real strengths exist across multiple
dimensions — but no single domain is fully formed,
and the domains are not yet reinforcing each other.

This pattern is most common at transition points:
a significant role change, a values evolution,
a life stage that has disrupted what previously worked.
Something has shifted, and the formation that served
you before is no longer sufficient for where you're going.

You have multiple genuine development needs competing
for limited attention and energy. Progress on all fronts
simultaneously produces progress on none.

The Fragmented pattern is not a verdict on your potential.
It is a description of your current structural state.
And structural states change — with concentrated effort
in the right place.

─── WHAT THIS COSTS YOU ────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: How this shows up.

Body:
You may feel like you're moving in several directions
at once without gaining real ground in any of them.

You likely have moments of strong performance —
followed by periods where nothing seems to click.
The inconsistency is frustrating precisely because
you know you're capable of the high-performance moments.

The work that feels most urgent is often not the work
that would move you most. Fragmented professionals
are particularly vulnerable to mistaking activity for progress.

─── YOUR FIRST MOVES ───────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: Where to start.

Move 1:
Look at your four domain scores. Identify the lowest.
That is where you start — not the domain you find most
interesting, not the one where you are closest to
breaking through. The lowest score is the structural
constraint on everything else.

Move 2:
For the next ninety days, concentrate exclusively
on that one domain. Not entirely — but every deliberate
development effort goes there. Concentration creates
the momentum that scattered effort cannot.

Move 3:
Resist the temptation to work on everything.
The Fragmented pattern resolves through sequencing,
not through simultaneous improvement.
Do one thing well before you return to the others.

─── UPGRADE BRIDGE ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What the full assessment reveals.

Body:
For the Fragmented pattern, the full assessment is
the most valuable of all five formations — because
the rapid classifier can identify the shape of the
fragmentation but cannot measure its depth.

The twenty sub-domain scores will tell you not just
which domain is lowest, but which specific sub-domain
within that domain is the actual constraint.
That precision converts a general direction into
a specific daily practice.

Your Formation Score, Alignment Index, and Structural
Integrity Index together will also tell you whether
this is a development challenge or a transition challenge —
which determines the timeframe and type of work required.

ARCHETYPE 05 — THE ALIGNED LEADER
Pattern: High scores across all four dimensions
Tagline: Compound formation.
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING RECOGNITION LINE

You've done the work. All of it.
The question now is what you do with that.

─── THE READING ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What this pattern means.

Body:
Your scores show strong development across all four
dimensions — and more importantly, they are reinforcing
each other. That compounding is the signature of
the Aligned Leader pattern.

This is the formation where growth in one dimension
accelerates growth in the others. Your self-knowledge
strengthens your character. Your character makes your
competence more reliable. Your competence creates
the platform for real impact.

You are not stuck in the way that brings most people
to this assessment. But you are likely at a threshold —
a point where the approach that got you here
is no longer the approach that takes you further.

The next form of growth is rarely more of what worked before.
It is usually a structural shift in how you operate.

─── WHAT THIS COSTS YOU ────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: How this shows up.

Body:
Aligned Leaders describe a specific tension:
they feel competent, purposeful, and effective —
and they feel a ceiling they cannot see clearly enough
to push through deliberately.

You may be asking how to scale what you've built.
How to multiply your impact through others rather
than through your own continued output.
How to move from being exceptional to being generative.

These are the right questions. They require
a different kind of diagnostic than the one
that got you to this pattern.

─── YOUR FIRST MOVES ───────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: Where to start.

Move 1:
Identify one person in your professional orbit who is
where you were three to five years ago. Invest in them
deliberately — not casually. The Aligned Leader's
next growth edge is almost always found in developing others.

Move 2:
Examine where your impact is personal and where it is
structural. Personal impact leaves when you do.
Structural impact — systems, cultures, capabilities
built into other people — persists. The question is
which kind you are primarily creating.

Move 3:
Consider what you are uniquely positioned to build
that would not exist without you — not a role,
but a contribution. Legacy work begins here.
You have the formation for it. The question is
whether you are directing it deliberately.

─── UPGRADE BRIDGE ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Header: What the full assessment reveals.

Body:
For Aligned Leaders, the full assessment is a precision
instrument rather than a diagnostic one.

Your Formation Score, Alignment Index, and Structural
Integrity Index will tell you not just that you are
well-formed, but exactly how the four dimensions
relate structurally — which pairing is driving
the compound effect, and where subtle imbalances
exist that are invisible at the rapid classifier level.

The sub-domain scores frequently reveal one or two
specific areas — often in Impact, sometimes in Character —
where additional depth would compound your existing
formation significantly.

The full assessment is also the foundation for
any coaching work. A practitioner working with
an Aligned Leader needs the complete picture —
not the pattern, but the precise structural map —
to work at the level this formation requires.

THE MOST IMPORTANT LINE ON THE PAGE
The opening recognition line must appear before the
framework explanation and before the domain scores.
It must be the first sentence they read after the
archetype name.

If a person reads that one line and thinks
"yes — exactly that" — everything else is a detail.
If they read it and feel nothing, the rest of the page
will not recover it.

This line is worth significant testing attention.
A/B test the opening recognition line per archetype
before testing any other element.

TONE ON THE RESULT PAGE
More direct than the landing page.
Less explanation of the framework — they already know it.
More precision about their specific situation.
The voice shifts from "let us show you" to
"here is what we found."

Trust them to handle truth plainly stated.
The people who take this assessment are not looking
to be managed. They are looking to be seen accurately.
