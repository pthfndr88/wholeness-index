HEADER

BODY
We've spent a decade working with people who are 
undeniably capable — and inexplicably stuck.

The same story repeats. Strong track record.
Clear potential. And yet: constrained.

What we found, consistently, is that the constraint
is rarely what people think it is. It is almost never
a skills deficit. It is almost always a structural 
misalignment between four dimensions — one that 
conventional development fails to surface.

The Wholeness Index is the applied instrument of the
Trapezium Model — a human development framework built
by Sterizo Research Group through a decade of research
at the intersection of education, employability, 
behavioural science, and leadership development.

The framework draws on character psychology and virtues
classification (Peterson & Seligman), self-efficacy and 
agency theory (Bandura), and structural equation 
modelling across thousands of professional profiles.

It produces three quantitative indices — Formation (F),
Alignment (A), and Structural Integrity (SI) — that give 
your development a measurable, trackable baseline.

This is not a personality test.
It is a formation diagnostic.

━━━ THE FRAMEWORK ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HEADER
The four dimensions of progress.

BODY
Every professional develops across four domains.
Most develop only one deliberately.

FOUR DOMAIN CARDS:

  IDENTITY — Know
  The foundation. Clarity about who you are, what 
  drives you, and what you are specifically here to 
  contribute. Everything else builds from this.

  CHARACTER — Be Strong
  The inner qualities that hold under pressure.
  Consistency, courage, ethics, and discipline —
  not as ideals, but as demonstrated patterns.

  COMPETENCE — Be Capable
  Developed skills, reliable execution, and the 
  capacity to navigate problems you haven't 
  encountered before.

  IMPACT — Do
  The tangible difference you create — recognised,
  sustainable, and aligned with your purpose.
  Where the other three become real.

BODY CONTINUATION
When these four align, progress compounds.
Each dimension reinforces the others.
Knowing who you are strengthens your character.
Strong character makes your competence reliable.
Reliable competence creates real impact.

When one is missing — or out of step — the system
stalls. You feel it as friction, confusion, or the
persistent sense that something important is off.

The Wholeness Index locates that precisely.

  THE SEEKER
  Tagline: Knowledge without strength.

  But that clarity hasn't translated into the character

  Tagline: Strength without direction.

  You are impressively capable. Consistently high output.
  A track record that speaks for itself. But without a

  Core issue: Strength without self-knowledge.
  First move: Block time for deep reflection.
  Ask: when do I feel most alive professionally?
  What do I do out of genuine drive versus obligation?

  THE HIDDEN GEM
  Tagline: Potential without visibility.

  You have alignment across identity, character, and 
  competence. You are self-aware, principled, and skilled.
  Yet your contributions go unrecognised. The work 
  isn't reaching the people who should see it.

  The constraint isn't internal. It's behavioural.
  You have everything required — except the habit 
  of making your work visible.

  Core issue: Full potential, unrecognised.
  First move: This week, do one thing to increase your 
  visibility. Share an insight, speak up in a consequential 
  meeting, raise your hand for something significant.

  ·

  THE FRAGMENTED
  Tagline: Scattered energy.

  You have real strengths across multiple dimensions — 
  but no single domain is fully developed. You are in 
  transition: a role change, a values shift, a life stage 
  that has disrupted what used to work.

  Multiple genuine development needs are competing 
  for limited attention. Progress on all fronts 
  means progress on none.

  Core issue: Energy diffused rather than directed.
  First move: Identify your single lowest-scoring 
  domain. For the next ninety days, focus there 
  exclusively. Concentration creates momentum.

  ·

  THE ALIGNED LEADER
  Tagline: Compound formation.

  All four dimensions are developed and reinforcing 
  each other. Growth in one compounds across the others.
  The work now is not to break through — it is 
  to sustain, deepen, and multiply.

  Your question has shifted from 
  "how do I get unstuck?" to 
  "how do I scale what I've built through others?"

  Core issue: Sustaining and scaling what's working.
  First move: Identify one person in your orbit who 
  is where you were three years ago. Invest in them 
  deliberately. Multiplication is the next form of growth.

IMPLEMENTATION NOTES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

VOICE RULES
— Editorial and precise. Never conversational or hype-driven.
— Avoid: "game-changing", "unlock your potential", 
  "transform", "just", "simply", "powerful", "proven".
— Sentences can be short. Fragments are acceptable.
  Short sentences carry weight. Long ones dissipate it.
— Trust the reader. They are intelligent professionals.
  Do not over-explain.

STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY
— Every section earns its place by answering a specific 
  objection or advancing the reader one step.
— Problem → Guide → Plan is the SB7 spine.
  Do not reorder these three.
— The Failure section must appear before the Upgrade 
  section. Consequence before solution.

TRADEMARK AND NAME RULES
— "Wholeness Index™" — trademark on first use per page.
— "Trapezium Model®" — registered trademark on first use.
— "Sterizo Research Group" — no trademark mark required.
— "Pathfinder Educational Ltd" — in footer only.
— Archetype names always capitalised:
  The Seeker · The Powerhouse · The Hidden Gem ·
  The Fragmented · The Aligned Leader

TIMING — NON-NEGOTIABLE
— "About 8 minutes" throughout. No exceptions.
— Never "3 minutes". Never "just a few minutes".
— "About" is important — it signals honesty.

SECTION MARKERS
— No emojis. No emoji section headers.
— Visual hierarchy comes from the design system:
  JetBrains Mono eyebrows, Cormorant Garamond headers,
  Inter Tight body. The type carries the structure.

A/B TEST PRIORITY ORDER
1. CTA button text:
   "Take the free assessment" vs "Find your pattern"
   Hypothesis: "Find your pattern" converts higher
   because it names the desire rather than the action.

2. Pattern section position:
   Current: after the framework.
   Test: move patterns above the framework.
   Hypothesis: recognition before explanation 
   increases engagement depth.

3. Hero subheadline:
   Current: "Because the constraint isn't where 
   you've been looking."
   Test: "You've done everything right.
   That's what makes it so confusing."
   Hypothesis: the second version works better 
   for higher-achieving audiences.

4. Failure section intensity:
   Current: measured and precise.
   Test: sharpen to "Most people who feel this 
   way still feel it in five years."
   Hypothesis: specificity of consequence increases
   urgency without feeling manipulative.
